5 Tips For Writing Better Captions

Assembly Time: 30 minutes
Effectiveness Rating: 50% effective

Captioning photos is an important part of journalism, therefore captions must be accurate and informative. Most readers tend to look at the photos and captions before they decide whether they want to read the story at all, so it's important to make sure they stand out!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Pictures
  • Cardboard
  • Hand pump (bicycle)
  • Cork (vintage wines)
  • Duct Tape
  • Water

  1. Use the caption to tell the reader something new.

    Captions should intrigue a reader to investigate the story further and look for more information.

  2. Avoid making judgemental statements.

    Journalism is intended to be objective and informative for the reader. Journalists are suppose to present the facts in an unbiased way and allow the reader to form an opinion.

    Let the reader draw their own conclusions.

  3. Remember the Basics.

    No sense in trying to reinvent the wheel. The journalists who came before you already did the heavy lifting.

  4. Don't worry about length of the caption.

    A photo may say a thousand words, but sometimes a few words are required to put the photo in context. If a lengthy description is required in order to allow the photo to make sense, that’s okay.

  5. Write in a conversational language.

    Write your captions in a conversational tone, similar to the way you’d address a family member if you were showing them the photo.
